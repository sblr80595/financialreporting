### Notes to the Disclosure:

**Depreciation on property, plant and equipment (refer note 4):** ₱ 4,25,53,104.38

This includes depreciation on all tangible fixed assets:
- Depreciation on transportation equipment: ₱ 40,12,075.16
- Depreciation on office equipment: ₱ 11,58,776.50
- Depreciation on laboratory equipment: ₱ 3,58,24,803.54
- Depreciation on furniture and fixtures: ₱ 5,327.62
- Depreciation on service tools: ₱ 10,61,121.56
- Amortization on leasehold improvements: ₱ 14,90,770.08

Depreciation is calculated based on the useful lives of assets as per Schedule II of the Companies Act, 2013 or as estimated by management. The depreciation method used is the straight-line method over the estimated useful life of each asset.

**Depreciation on right of use assets (refer note 4A):** ₱ 38,91,727.02

This represents the depreciation charge on right-of-use assets recognized under Ind AS 116 (Leases). Right-of-use assets are depreciated over the shorter of the lease term or the useful life of the underlying asset.

**Amortisation of intangible assets (refer note 5A):** ₱ 14,33,033.65

This includes amortization of the following intangible assets:
- Amortization of software: ₱ 4,22,009.97
- Amortization of Microsoft Office and licenses: ₱ 10,11,023.68

Intangible assets are amortized over their estimated useful lives or the period of expected economic benefit, typically over 3 to 5 years depending on the nature of the asset.

All depreciation and amortisation expenses are presented as non-cash expenses in the profit and loss statement and are allocated to the relevant functional categories based on the assets' use in operations.
