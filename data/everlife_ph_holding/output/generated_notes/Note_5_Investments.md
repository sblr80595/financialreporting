### Notes to the Financial Statements

This note discloses investments recognized in the balance sheet as per Ind AS 28 - Investments in Associates and Joint Ventures.

**Investments in Associates:**

The Company holds investments in Lifeline Daignostic Inc., which is classified as an associate. Associates are entities over which the company has significant influence but not control. Significant influence is the power to participate in the financial and operating policy decisions of the investee but is not control or joint control over those policies.

**Accounting Treatment:**

- Investments in associates are initially recognized at cost
- Subsequently measured using the equity method as per Ind AS 28
- Under equity method, the investment is adjusted for:
  * Share of profit or loss of the associate
  * Share of other comprehensive income of the associate
  * Dividends received from the associate (reduces investment value)
  * Impairment losses (if any)

**Presentation Notes:**

1. Investments in associates are non-current assets
2. Presented as positive amounts representing the carrying value
3. Measured using equity method under Ind AS 28
4. Carrying amount as at the balance sheet date: ₹64,00,00,000
5. Follows Schedule III format for balance sheet presentation

**Schedule III Compliance:**

This format follows Schedule III of Companies Act, 2013 requirements for investments disclosure. Additional disclosures may be required in detailed financial statements:

- Name of each associate: Lifeline Daignostic Inc.
- Percentage of ownership interest
- Country of incorporation
- Principal activities
- Share of profit/loss and OCI for the year
- Fair value of investment (if publicly traded)
- Summarized financial information of associates
- Nature and extent of significant restrictions
- Unrecognized share of losses (if applicable)
- Commitments and contingent liabilities related to associates

**Ind AS 28 Disclosure Requirements:**

Further disclosures as per Ind AS 28 would include:
- List of associates with proportion of ownership interest
- Summarized financial information of material associates
- Nature of the relationship with the associate
- Reasons for classifying as associate
- Reporting date differences (if any)
- Restrictions on ability of associates to transfer funds
