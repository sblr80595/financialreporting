### Nature and Description

This note discloses cash and cash equivalents recognized in the balance sheet as per Ind AS 7 - Statement of Cash Flows.

**Bank Balance**: Cash and cash equivalents comprise cash at bank and on hand, and short-term deposits with an original maturity of three months or less, which are subject to an insignificant risk of changes in value. The balance includes:
- Cash in Bank - RCBC: ₹51,240.57
- Cash in Bank - CITI: ₹13,86,931.78

**Definition as per Ind AS 7:**
Cash equivalents are held for the purpose of meeting short-term cash commitments rather than for investment or other purposes. For an investment to qualify as a cash equivalent, it must be readily convertible to a known amount of cash and be subject to an insignificant risk of changes in value.

**Presentation Notes:**
1. Cash and cash equivalents are current assets
2. Presented as positive amounts (debit balances)
3. Highly liquid and readily available for use
4. Include only balances available for immediate use
5. Exclude restricted cash or deposits with maturity > 3 months
6. Follow Schedule III format for balance sheet presentation

**Schedule III Compliance:**
This format follows Schedule III of Companies Act, 2013 requirements for cash and cash equivalents disclosure. The balances represent funds readily available for operational and strategic purposes.

**Ind AS 7 Requirements:**
- Cash and cash equivalents are readily convertible to known amounts of cash
- Original maturity is 3 months or less
- Subject to insignificant risk of changes in value
- Reconciliation between balance sheet and cash flow statement maintained
